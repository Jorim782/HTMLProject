Cyber Security

Malware

A malware attack is any malicious software intended to harm or destroy a computer, server, client, computer network, and/or infrastructure without the knowledge of the end user.

Process

Malware is created, utilised, and sold by online criminals for a variety of purposes, although it is most typically used to steal personal, financial, or business data. Cyber attackers almost usually concentrate their tactics, methods, and procedures (TTPs) on obtaining privileged credentials and accounts in order to complete their task, despite the fact that their motivations can differ.
There are many types of malware attacks such as viruses, worms and trojan. A computer virus can replicate itself and is built to spread from host to host. Computer viruses cannot replicate and propagate without programming like a file or document, much to how viruses cannot multiply without a host cell. Whereas A worm has the ability to duplicate itself without the assistance of the end user and can quickly spread from one system to another, infecting whole networks. And trojan malware is one of the most challenging kinds of malware to find since it impersonates a trustworthy program. When executed by the victim, this kind of malware's malicious code and instructions can function covertly. It is frequently employed to allow different kinds of malware into the system.
